Comcast Business - Mobile App

Information Architecture, Wireframming, Mid-fi protoyping, User testing

Project Details
Challenge: Combine two large ecosystems used by Comcast Business partners at the small, medium and pre-enterprise level.

Company: Comcast

Role: UX Designer
Contribution
Information Architecture
Personas
Wireframming
Mid-fi protoyping
User testing
Tools Used
Pen and Paper
Sketch
Invision

This mobile app combined two large ecosystems. One was a complete voice functionality, including voice-to-text functionality and call transfer.

The second ecosystem involved adapting existing web based account functionality. This inherent disparity between the two ecosystems created complexities when combining them.

From the onset, the team suggested to maintain parity between these two ecosystems, in the form of an app suite. We started by embarking on an extensive research of current in market apps from competing companies and similar industries to build the case for a two app approach.



Monolithic Vs App suite

It became clear that there were broader politics at play within Comcast. There was a clear need for a mobile solution for both voice and account management functionality from a market perspective. Comcast had to act fast.

Every major player in Comcast's space had a similar app in market. Most however, offered a suite of apps. Comcast Business wanted one.

After nearly a month of research and extensive strategy illuminating the strengths and weaknesses of a monolithic app versus an app suite, the Comcast team made the decision to pursue a single-app solution.


Approach


Comcast had major priorities and functionality requirements already defined by major stakeholders across the broader team and divisions involved in this initiative.

Personas

The project benefited from personas crafted and refined from previous projects, as well as client history. Additional persona based research for this initiative involved coloring these personas to include business-focused characteristics.


Roadmap

The mobile app should simply replicate the functionality that currently existed in the online portal,


Process


We were engaged throughout the entire process of research, discovery, ideation, building, prototyping and testing the app

Competitive analysis

Conducted extensive research on current in market solutions from other company's self service mobile apps spanning the telecommunication industry and apps from other industries.

Initial workflows, sketches and concepts laid the foundation for the ecosystem to come.

The team mapped Comcast business partner's every touchpoint to understand how user's think, feel, and behave.


Information Architecture

We Made sure that the architecture and chosen navigation schema was good, as it was the foundational step before significant investment in sketching and wireframing commenced.


Sketching

Sketching out ideas and testing them against our workflows and architecture, as well as quickly testing and validating core concepts with other designers, laid the foundation for our wireframing and prototyping.


Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing


All of our wireframes and prototypes were done using Sketch and Invision.


Bring it all together

The first round release was a soft iOS launch to key business partners and internal staff. The second round release included Android and was available for all Comcast business partners.


User testing

We conducted over 20 user tests divided into two stages of testing, and compared completion rates and overall experiences.


Launch

The app launch was proof that a launch is only the beginning. We successfully launched a product that offered much needed experiences for their business customers, while giving Comcast a presence in the business mobile space.
