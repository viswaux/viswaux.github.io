Data Collection & Research


Data Collection

Data included:

  • Spreadsheets of data collected from user interviews re: app usage
  • Information on how access is given based on the employee roles (exempt vs non-exempt vs managers)
  • Various titles and job codes to differentiate roles
Research

We walked through scenarios in various employee roles (employee, manager, HR) to get an understanding of how the employee would interact with the app. This also helped us to determine the core component to differentiate each persona based on their role.

We tried to find articles on building out employee personas. Much of the literature was for general persona development. There isn’t a ton of research on building employee personas. Hopefully this can add to the User Persona Canon!


Wireframing & Ideation


We took our initial sketches and started creating different persona wireframes using Balsamiq. Our initial wireframes had 3 different personas based on what the research demonstrated. There were different type of employee roles accessing the employee portal.

Our focus was less on placement and design and more on ensuring we have the required content.

  • Employee – Hourly – non-exempt employees who are paid by the hour
  • Employee – Salary – exempt employees who do not have direct reports
  • Employee – Reports – exempt employees with direct reports

We broke down each potential role and how the employee differed in the systems they would access. We documented eveything in a spreadsheet.


Updated versions and ensuring represented diversity


After more design meetings we decided that there would be 5 priority persona roles:

  • Candidate - a new hire that hasn't started yet
  • Employee - Hourly (2) - one hourly was managing HR apps
  • Employee - Salary 
  • Employee - Direct Reports

Diversity

Before sending our designs to the UI/Visual Developer on the team, we threw on some images pulled from Unsplash and Pixabay to ensure that the diversity we were seeking would be represented. We also tried to represent this in the names we chose: Silvio, Haripal, Helena. Comcast represents 95,000 employees across the country and that diversity needed to be represented.


Next steps

We are still in the process of building out all the personas, but am excited to be CLOSER to the final stages of having Employee Personas that represents 95,000 employees.
